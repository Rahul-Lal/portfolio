During my time as a Computer Power Plus student, I learned various programming languages from SQL, Java and C#. Other areas such as database development, SDLC, and project management. I also learned things outside of my course, such as dressing to impress, finding direction from expierence (in general), and other tricks that could help impress an employer. By late July, I had completed my diploma. I didn't decided not to find work immediately, not before I gain some work expierence. As a ex-school graduate who completed a IT diploma, finding work will be a drag. So gaining prior expierence would expose me to the real-world enviroment, expand my skills, connect with other in industry. On my last attempt, I didn't have enough skills to be exposed to real-world expierence. So I chose to continue study.

On early April, I started studying the Diploma in Distributed Software Development at AMES IT.
