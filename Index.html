About Myself

My name is Rahul Lal. I'm pursuing a career as a software developer

My career path began in early 2011, the year I started NCEA Level 1 at Pakuranga College. I was placed in a generic computing course to complete my school timetable. I was amazed by the content that people would think they could do in their sleep. This content consists of touch-typing, understandig the Microsoft Office Suite, and basic HTML and CSS. I continued to take the classes for my last two years of school. That got me to chose to have a career in Information Technology.

A few months after graduating from college, I started studying the Diploma in Software Development at Computer Power Plus. I chose Computer Power Plus as oppose to university, as they focus on skill than academics, self-pace & directed learning as oppose to lectures & labs, and to build on my current skillset at that time.

During my time as a Computer Power Plus student, I learned various programming languages from SQL, Java and C#. Other areas such as database development, SDLC, and project management. I also learned things outside of my course, such as dressing to impress, finding direction from expierence (in general), and other tricks that could help impress an employer. I was also out of comfort zone, surrounded with students who are older than me and have expierence.

By late July, I had completed my diploma. I didn't decided not to find work immediately, not before I gain some work expierence. As a ex-school graduate who completed a IT diploma, finding work will be a drag. So gaining prior expierence would expose me to the real-world enviroment, expand my skills, connect with other in industry. On my last attempt, I didn't have enough skills to be exposed to real-world expierence. So I chose to continue study.

On early April, I started studying the Diploma in Distributed Software Development at AMES IT.
